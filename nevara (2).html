The challenge

Restoration happens. Proof doesn't keep up.

Organizations are doing genuine, meaningful ecological work — restoring wetlands, reviving lakes, rebuilding forests. But when a funder, a government body, or an auditor asks for evidence, the reality is scattered photos, disconnected reports, and no unified record.

01 · Documentation

The evidence is everywhere — and nowhere

Field photos on WhatsApp. Annual reports in different folders. Observations never organized. When stakeholders ask for proof of progress, assembling it takes weeks.

02 · Monitoring

You can't visit every site, every month

Restoration projects span years and hectares. Without continuous monitoring, you lose visibility into what's changing — until it's too late to course-correct or document.

03 · Reporting

Funders want data. You have stories.

Donor reviews, SDG alignment documents, government audits, ESG disclosures — all need structured, verifiable records. Turning field observations into institutional-grade reports takes more time than the work itself.
